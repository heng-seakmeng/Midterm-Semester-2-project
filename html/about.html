THE BEGINNING

From a Garage in Surrey to the Front of the Grid

Bruce McLaren founded the team in 1963 with a simple conviction: that a small, obsessive group of engineers could beat the establishment on talent alone. The first McLaren-built car raced within a year. Within a decade, the papaya livery was a fixture at the front of Formula 1.

That same restless engineering culture now defines every road car we build. Each one carries technology and discipline developed on the track, refined for the street, and obsessed over until it earns the badge.
